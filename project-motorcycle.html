Introduction

These bikes are practical engineering and electronics projects, not industrial design focused. I completed the first one over 10 years ago and it has been road registered and used as a commuter in Nelson and Christchurch. The farm bike was completed in mid 2025 and is currently being used on my parents high country farm in Marlborough.

Both bikes have been built from individual components and have increased my electronics knowledge hugely. I also taught myself to weld and machine through building these.

More second column text.

I have a strong interest in transport design, especially electric. The farm bike has totally convinced me that farm bikes are a great application for electric power. My bike has a battery pack I built myself, from nearly 400 batteries, spot welded together with a welder I built myself from a microwave transformer. Yet it has good power, no clutch or gears, regeneration down hills and no noise from an engine or a flopping chain.
